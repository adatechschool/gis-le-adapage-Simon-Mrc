The Recent Rise Of Climbing

Proliferation of climbing gyms

Over the past decade, climbing gyms have proliferated rapidly, transforming a once niche sport into a mainstream urban activity. Fueled by growing interest in fitness, community-oriented spaces, and Olympic exposure, these gyms are popping up in cities large and small.

Finding and starting at a nearby climbing gym is easier than it might seem. A quick online search or map app will usually reveal gyms close to you, along with reviews and beginner information. Most gyms offer intro classes, gear rentals, and friendly staff who can help you get started, so there’s no need to own equipment or have prior experience.

The benefits of climbing

Climbing offers a wide range of benefits, combining physical, mental, and social rewards in a single activity. It builds full-body strength, balance, flexibility, and endurance while also sharpening problem-solving skills and focus. Mentally, climbing can reduce stress and boost confidence as climbers overcome challenges and track their progress. At the same time, it fosters a strong sense of community, making it both a powerful workout and an engaging, motivating way to stay active.

Some personnalities to follow

Magnus Midtbø is a Norwegian professional climber and widely known climbing content creator. Formerly one of the world’s top competition climbers, he later shifted his focus to outdoor climbing and media, gaining a massive following on YouTube. Through videos that mix elite-level performance, science-based training insights, and entertaining challenges, he has played a major role in popularizing climbing and making the sport more accessible to a global audience.

Adam Ondra is a Czech professional climber and one of the most accomplished climbers in history. Known for his exceptional strength, flexibility, and technical skill, he has pushed the sport’s limits with groundbreaking ascents in both competition and outdoor climbing. As the first person to climb a route graded 9c and a dominant force in international competitions, Ondra is widely regarded as a defining figure in modern climbing.

Oriane Bertone is a French professional rock climber, born on March 10, 2005, on Réunion Island. She is considered one of the most talented young climbers in the world, especially in bouldering. Oriane gained international attention at a very young age by winning several youth world titles and quickly moving into senior competitions. She has won multiple medals in IFSC Climbing World Cup events and is known for her strength, technique, and determination. Bertone is also part of the new generation of athletes helping to popularize sport climbing, especially after its inclusion in the Olympic Games.

Alex Megos is a German professional rock climber, born on August 12, 1993. He is best known for his exceptional performances in lead climbing and for being one of the strongest sport climbers in the world. In 2013, he made history by becoming the first climber to onsight a 9a (5.14d) route, Estado Crítico in Spain. Megos has also had great success in international competitions, winning multiple medals at IFSC World Cups and World Championships. He is admired for his precision, consistency, and calm approach to climbing at the highest level.

Janja Garnbret is a Slovenian professional sport climber, born on March 12, 1999. She is widely regarded as the greatest competition climber of all time. Specializing in both lead climbing and bouldering, Janja has dominated the international scene with numerous IFSC World Cup wins and multiple World Championship titles. She became Olympic champion at the Tokyo 2020 Games and is known for her incredible consistency, creativity on the wall, and mental strength. Garnbret has set a new standard in sport climbing and continues to inspire athletes around the world.
